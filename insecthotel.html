What Is an Insect Hotel?

An insect hotel‚Äîalso called a bug hotel, insect house, or bug box‚Äîis a man-made shelter built using natural materials like bamboo, wood, straw, and leaves. These shelters mimic natural hiding and nesting spots for beneficial insects.

What‚Äôs Inside an Insect Hotel?

  • Bamboo Canes & Hollow Stems: For solitary bees like mason and leafcutter bees.
  • Drilled Logs: Mimic natural holes for bees to lay eggs.
  • Pinecones & Twigs: Hiding spots for ladybugs and beetles.
  • Dry Leaves & Straw: Nesting areas for lacewings and small bugs.
  • Clay or Mud Sections: Used by mud-building bees and wasps.
  • Protective Roof: Keeps everything dry and safe from the rain.

Pollinators: Tiny Heroes with a Big Job

  • Over 75% of flowering plants depend on pollinators like bees.
  • Solitary bees are highly effective pollinators.
  • Pollination helps plants grow, bloom, and produce food.

Why Are Insect Hotels Good for the Garden?

  • Ladybugs: Eat aphids that damage plants.
  • Ground Beetles: Eat slugs and caterpillars.
  • Decomposers: Improve soil by breaking down organic matter.

With more helpful insects, we can reduce or eliminate the need for pesticides and keep our garden naturally healthy.

Our insect hotels are made from reused and eco-friendly materials. They support our values of environmental care and responsible living.

  • Promote biodiversity in our schoolyard.
  • Teach hands-on eco-learning to students.

Who Lives in Our Insect Hotel?

Ladybugs: Beneficial insects that help control aphid populations naturally.

Bees: Essential pollinators that help flowers and crops thrive.

Butterflies: Beautiful pollinators that indicate a healthy environment.
